Splendor Duel Rules





**SETUP**



Follow these steps in order:



Step 1. Shuffle the 3 decks of Jewel cards separately, then place them in a column, with the deck of level 1 cards at the bottom.



Step 2  Reveal: 3 level-3 cards, 4 level-2 cards, and 5 level-1 cards to form a pyramid.



Step 3  Place the board below this pyramid and randomly place the 25 tokens on it, starting with the central space and following the printed spiral.



Step 4  Place the 3 Privilege scrolls above the board.



Step 5  Place the 4 Royal cards below the board.



Step 6  Choose the first player randomly. Their opponent takes 1 Privilege and places it in front of themself.





**GAME OVERVIEW**



The players take turns, one after another, until one player wins the game.



To win, you must fulfil one of the three conditions listed at the end of this rulebook.





**PLAYER TURN**



1. Optional Actions

2\. Mandatory Actions

3\. Royal Card Check

4\. End of Turn Sequence





**Phase 1: Optional Actions**



You first perform none, one or both of the follow optional actions (in the indicated order).



1\. Use a Privilege Scroll - Return 1 or more Privilege Scrolls to their place above the board. For each Privilege Scroll returned, take 1 Gem or Pearl token of your choice from the board. This action cannot be used to take a Gold token.



2\. Replenish the Game Board - You cannot perform this action if the bag is empty. Shuffle the tokens in the bag and place them on empty spaces, starting with the central space and following the printed spiral until the bag is empty. Then, as a penalty for the player who replenished, your opponent takes 1 Privilege Scroll from the board (if there are no Privilege Scrolls above the board, they take one Privilege Scroll from you) (if your opponent already has a 3 Privilege Scrolls, ignore this effect). If you would not be able to perform any of the mandatory actions, you must perform the "Replenish the Game Board" optional action before choosing your mandatory action.





**Phase 2: Mandatory Actions**



You must perform one of the following three mandatory actions:



**1. Take up to 3 adjacent Gem and/or Pearl tokens from the board** - All Gem and/or Pearl tokens taken must form a single uninterrupted (by gold tokens or empty spaces) horizontal, vertical or diagonal line. You can take 2 adjacent Gem and/or Pearl tokens or even a single Gem and/or Pearl token. This action cannot be used to take a Gold token. If you use this action to take 3 tokens of the same color or 2 Pearls, your opponent takes 1 Privilege Scroll.



**2. Take 1 Gold Token from the board and reserve 1 card** - To reserve a card either: take 1 Jewel card of your choice from the pyramid OR draw the top card from one of the 3 decks (level 1, level 2, or level 3). When you reserve a card from the pyramid, replace it with a new card from the corresponding deck, placing it faceup in the pyramid. If the corresponding deck is empty, the card is not replaced. You cannot perform this action if there are no Gold tokens on the board. You cannot perform this action if you already have 3 reserved cards. You cannot have more than 3 reserved cards. Reserved cards are kept secret from your opponent (either facedown in front of you, or in your hand). Your opponent may see the number of reserved cards which you have. You may look at your reserved cards at any time.



**3. Purchase 1 Jewel Card** - Choose a card from the pyramid or your reserve, pay its cost in tokens, and place that card faceup in front of you. Gold tokens are wild and can be used in place of any Gem or Pearl token. Place spent tokens in the bag. It is possible to have enough bonuses to reduce the cost of a card to 0 tokens. The cost of a card may not be reduced below 0, meaning you cannot gain tokens when purchasing cards. When you purchase a card from the pyramid, replace it with a new card from the corresponding level deck, placing it faceup in the pyramid. After you purchase a Jewel Card, if it has a card ability, resolve that ability now.





**Phase 3: Royal Cards**



Some Jewel Cards have Crowns. If you purchased Jewel Cards during the Mandatory Actions phase, check if you have passed the 3 Crowns or 6 Crowns threshold.



If you have reached your 3rd crown, you must take (your choice) one of the available Royal cards and resolve its ability.

If you have reached your 6th crown, you must take (your choice) one of the available Royal cards and resolve its ability.

No further Royal cards are awarded beyond the 6th Crown milestone.



Place your Royal cards next to your Jewel cards. 





**Phase 4: End of Turn Sequence**



Follow the End of Turn sequence.



**1. Victory Check** - Check if you have fulfilled any of the following three victory conditions. If you have, the game immediately ends and you win!



\- If you have 20 or more Prestige points in total.

\- If you have 10 or more Prestige points on cards of the same color (white, blue, green, red or black). Null is not a color.

\- If you have 10 or more Crowns.



**2. Discard Check** - Count your Gem, Pearl and Gold tokens. If you have more than 10 tokens in total, you must discard down to 10 tokens. Choose which ones to discard and return them to the bag.



3\. Your opponent's turn begins (unless you played a Jewel Card or Royal Card with the Turn ability, in which case your turn begins instead).

**CARD ABILITIES**



Jewel Cards and Royal Cards have abilities. 



**Privilege:** Take 1 Privilege scroll from the board. If none are available, take 1 from your opponent. If you already have 3 Privilege scrolls, ignore this effect.



**Take:** Take 1 Gem or Pearl token from your opponent. This is mandatory if your opponent has any eligible tokens. If your opponent has no Gem or Pearl tokens, ignore this effect. You cannot take a Gold token from your opponent.



**Token:** Take 1 gem token matching the color of this card from the board. If there are no such gem tokens left, ignore this effect.



**Turn:** The next turn is yours again and not your opponent's turn. If more than one Turn ability is played in a single turn, ignore this effect. Turn effects do not stack.



**Wild:** You cannot purchase a Jewel Card with the wild ability unless you have a Jewel Card that has a color (null is not a color). The card with the wild ability permanently becomes the colour of any Jewel Card with a colour which you have. This color assignment is permanent and cannot be changed.
